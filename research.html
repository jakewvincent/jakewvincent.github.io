Processing of extraction from islands
It is well known that cases of extraction from syntactic islands have gradient (un)acceptability. Research on the processing side has found that when the parser is actively anticipating possible gap sites with which to associate a filler phrase, it generally does not posit gap sites inside of islands. I am investigating whether these findings generalize to more acceptable examples involving extraction from islands, like those shown below, from Chung and McCloskey (1983: 708).

Other research

Rhyme in Chamorro poetry
In a recent project, I analyzed 106 of Joaquin F. Borja's rhyme pairs from the collection of Chamorro literature Istreyas Marianas: Chamorro. Approximately 25% of the rhyme pairs there have strict rhyme (= identical from the vowel of the stressed syllable to the end of the word), and the remainder have what I called abstract rhyme, which seems to creatively utilize abstract associations between classes of segments established by the phonological processes in Chamorro grammar. The use of abstract rhyme results in a broad (yet orderly) range of licit rhyming pairs. (handout)
